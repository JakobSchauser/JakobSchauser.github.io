About Me

I am currently a PhD fellow in TrusinaLab in the Biocomplexity Group at the Niels Bohr Institute in Copenhagen. My research focuses on how elementary physical principles can lead to interesting biological phenomena.

My work involves using modeling to understand the many emergent properties of the incredible natural world. Nature consists of simple rules and complex behaviors - and, importantly, these are not opposites!

Recently, I have been exploring Neural Cellular Automata as a tool for understanding large-scale reverberation of local dynamics.

Outside of work, I love movies, music, games, and books. Any recommendations are appreciated!
