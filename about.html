About Me

I am currently a PhD fellow in TrusinaLab in the Biocomplexity Group at the Niels Bohr Institute in Copenhagen. My research focuses on how simple physical principles can lead to complex biological phenomena.

My work involves using modeling to understand the many emergent properties of the incredible natural world.

Recently, I have been exploring Neural Cellular Automata as a tool for understanding the interplay between local and global dynamics.

I love movies, music, games, and books. Any recommendations are appreciated!
